About Me

PhD Scholar in Hydraulics at IIT Gandhinagar

I am a PhD research scholar in Civil Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Gandhinagar, specializing in Hydraulics under the guidance of Prof. Pranab Kumar Mohapatra. My research focuses on the complex fluid dynamics of vortex drop shafts, which I investigate using a combination of experimental methods and advanced numerical modeling (CFD).

My doctoral studies are complemented by hands-on involvement in significant water management projects. I have contributed to the Water4Change project, an Indo-Dutch initiative focused on creating solutions for urban water challenges, and am actively working on the cNarmada initiative, which aims to develop an integrated river basin management plan.

Research Interests

Vortex Drop Shafts Urban Hydraulics CFD Multiphase Flow Water Sensitive Urban Design (WSUD) River Basin Management

Current Questions I am Working On:

  • How can we optimize energy dissipation in vortex drop shafts handling extreme urban storm flows?
  • What are the most accurate multiphase CFD modeling approaches for complex hydraulic structures?
  • How can we seamlessly integrate localized hydraulic data into broader river basin management plans?

Ongoing Research & Projects

PhD Research: Vortex Drop Shafts

Objective: To investigate and optimize the complex multiphase fluid dynamics and energy dissipation mechanisms within vortex drop shafts used in urban drainage.

Methods: 3D Computational Fluid Dynamics (FLOW-3D HYDRO), scaled experimental physical modeling.

Key Focus: Characterizing flow regimes and evaluating pressure distributions under extreme flow scenarios.

Ground Response Analysis

Objective: Analyze the seismic ground response of soil deposits at the IIT Patna campus under various earthquake motions.

Methods: 1D equivalent linear analysis using DEEPSOIL software.

Key Result: Quantified the correlation between shear wave velocity profiles and peak ground acceleration (PGA) amplification.
